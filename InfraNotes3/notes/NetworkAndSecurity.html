2025-11-24 and beyond: Networking and Security

Network operations

Network

A network is when multiple devices are connected to each other, either wirelessly or wired.
They either share data with each other or share power with each other.


They require these aspects:

  • Connection
  • Communicate
  • Resources

Network Types

Local Area Network (LAN)
Covers a small area like home or a school Lab
Wire Are Network (WAN
Connect multiple LANs in large areas like between cities
The Internet
Global network of networks

Network Hardware

  1. Network Interface Card (NIC)
    Allows a computer to connect to a network, can be wired or wireless
  2. Switch
    Connect devices in the same local network, sends packets of data
  3. Router
    Connect to networks. Forwards packets to other networks (e.g., your ISP (your internet Provider)
  4. Modem
    Convert data from and to your ISP
  5. Transport medium
    Transport your data across the ISP or other providers Network

How networks work?

A network is like a postal system


Data = letters

IP addresses = Street addresses

Routers = post offices

Protocols = rules of delivery


Internet Service Providers (ISP)

Your modem connects to your internet service provider (ISP)

  • Bel, Videotron, etc...
  • We pay to connect to their ISPs, hardware they maintain and operate

ISPs connect to even bigger networks called

Network Service Providers (NSPs)

NSPs provide access to the “internet backbone”, the high speed “highways” of the internet

How Networks work: Data (Packet), The letters

When you send data (your letter) over a network (webpage, email, file), you computer
doesn’t send it as one big piece. Instead, it breaks the data into small units called packets
each line of your letter being sent separately).

Each packet contains:

  • Header -> Control information (source & destination IP, sequence number, protocol)
  • Payloud -> Actual file data which is carried

This is basically your message, your mail or letter you wabt to send to a friend


How Networks work: IP addresses, The street address

IP (internet protocol) address is an unique identifier for a device on a network

An IP address is like the home address on a letter for your computer


Two Versions:

  • IPv4: 32-bit
  • IPv6: 128-bit

They are the addresses, the doors to all of the devices

How Networks work: Routers, The post office

They are like the post offices
They can connect letters/data to other places to be sent or recieved

  1. They receive the packet
  2. They store the data
  3. They forwarded the data

Just like how they recieve mail or letters and send them!


How Networks work: Protocols, Transportation

A protocol is a set of rules that devices follow to communicate over a network


It is like a language, if devices don’t follow the same rules. They can’t understand each other
Most communication on the internet uses layers of protocols.

For Example:
Writing a letter (HTTP), putting it in an envelope, and sending it (TCP) etc.
